Goals

Advancing Biomedical Engineering Education

The IEEE Enis Student Branch's Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society (EMBS) Chapter aims to advance biomedical engineering among students and foster connections between academia and industry in healthcare technology.

Bridging Academia and Industry

EMBS leverages engineering principles to tackle challenges in medicine and biology, aiming to enhance healthcare outcomes.

Supporting Members' Professional Growth

We offer workshops, competitions, recognition programs, and networking events to support members' interests in this dynamic field.

The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE)

is the world's largest professional association for advancement of technology. It connects students, researchers, and industry professionals in various engineering fields.

The National School of Engineers of Sfax (ENIS)

Established in 1983, is a powerhouse for education, research, and technological advancement in Tunisia. This esteemed institution equips students with the knowledge and skills to become engineers across seven distinct disciplines: electrical, electromechanical, computer, materials, civil, biological, and geo-resources and environmental engineering

Joining the IEEE ENIS Student Branch at the National School of Engineers of Sfax (ENIS)

Joining the IEEE ENIS Student Branch at the National School of Engineers of Sfax (ENIS) unlocks a treasure trove of benefits for students. From career development to fostering a sense of community, the branch empowers you to excel both professionally and personally. Networking opportunities abound, allowing you to connect with industry professionals, potential employers, and fellow ENIS students passionate about engineering and technology. These connections can prove invaluable when seeking internships, jobs, or simply building a strong support network throughout your academic journey.
